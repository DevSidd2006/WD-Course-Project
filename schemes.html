Agricultural Schemes

  • Pradhan Mantri KISAN Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)

    The Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN) Yojana is a farmer welfare scheme launched by the Government of India. Under this scheme, the government provides a direct benefit of Rs 6,000 per year to all small and marginal farmers in the country. This benefit is provided in three installments every 4 months. The objective of the PM-KISAN scheme is to promote farmers' income and make them self-reliant.

    Read More

  • Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana

    The Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) is a crop insurance scheme launched by the Government of India in 2016. The scheme aims to provide financial protection to farmers against crop losses due to natural calamities, pests, and diseases. The scheme is mandatory for all farmers cultivating notified crops in notified areas. The premium for the scheme is shared between the government and the farmers.

    It provides financial protection to farmers against crop losses due to natural calamities, pests, and diseases. It helps to reduce farmers' vulnerability to crop losses, improve their income, stabilize the agricultural sector, and promote sustainable agriculture.

    Read More

  • Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana

    The Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY) is a centrally sponsored scheme launched in 2015. The scheme aims to provide financial assistance to farmers for the installation of micro-irrigation systems. It is open to all farmers who own less than 2 hectares of land. The financial assistance is provided in the form of a loan, which is interest-free for the first two years.

    It helps to increase the adoption of micro-irrigation systems, improve water use efficiency, increase crop production, reduce production costs, and improve farmers' income and standard of living.

    Read More

  • Ayushman Sahakar Scheme

    The Ayushman Sahakar Scheme is a government-funded program launched in 2023 with a budget of Rs. 10,000 crore. It provides financial assistance to cooperative societies to improve health infrastructure, including construction and renovation of hospitals, purchase of medical equipment, training of medical staff, and promotion of preventive healthcare.

    It aims to improve the quality of healthcare in rural and underserved areas, reduce the financial burden on poor communities, promote preventive healthcare, and create jobs in the healthcare sector.

    Read More

  • E-NAM (National Agriculture Market)

    eNAM, or the National Agriculture Market, is a pan-India electronic trading portal launched on April 14, 2016, to network existing APMC mandis and create a unified national market for agricultural commodities. It addresses challenges like lack of transparency, high transaction costs, and limited market access.

    It provides real-time price discovery, reduces transaction costs, increases market access, improves infrastructure, and promotes transparency and accountability.

    Read More

  • Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maan-Dhan Yojana (PM-KMY)

    Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maandhan Yojana (PM-KMY) is a voluntary pension scheme launched on 12 September 2019 for small and marginal farmers. It provides a guaranteed monthly pension of Rs. 3,000 after age 60 for farmers with up to 2 hectares of land, with contributions matched by the government.

    Farmers contribute Rs. 55 to Rs. 200 monthly, and the government matches up to Rs. 1,000. The scheme ensures financial security in old age.

    Read More

  • Krishi Kalyan Abhiyan

    Krishi Kalyan Abhiyan (KKA), launched in 2018, aims to improve farmers' income and livelihood in 112 aspirational districts. Activities include distributing soil health cards, livestock vaccination, mini kits, custom hiring centres, organic farming, and farmer training.

    It has trained over 11 lakh farmers, conducted 5000 demonstrations, and improved soil health on 15 lakh hectares.

    Read More

  • Soil Health Cards (SHC) Scheme

    The Soil Health Card (SHC) Scheme, launched in 2015, provides farmers with soil nutrient analysis and recommendations to improve soil health and productivity. Soil samples are tested for 12 parameters, including nitrogen, phosphorus, and pH.

    It helps farmers identify nutrient deficiencies, save on fertilizers, increase crop productivity, and protect the environment.

    Read More

  • National Bamboo Mission

    The National Bamboo Mission (NBM) promotes bamboo cultivation and industry development. It provides financial assistance for plantations, nurseries, farmer training, and bamboo-based industries.

    It has brought over 1 lakh hectares under bamboo cultivation, trained 1 lakh farmers, and created 10,000 jobs.

    Read More

  • Green Revolution – Krishonnati Yojana

    Launched in 2005, the Krishonnati Yojana aims to uplift agriculture through 11 schemes, including horticulture development, sustainable agriculture, mechanization, and e-governance, to improve farmer incomes and production.

    It focuses on scientific approaches, nutritional security, and agricultural marketing infrastructure.

    Read More

  • Pradhan Mantri Annadata Aay SanraksHan Abhiyan (PM-AASHA)

    Launched in 2018, PM-AASHA provides price support to farmers for crops like wheat, rice, pulses, and oilseeds, ensuring a minimum price based on production costs and market prices.

    It benefits around 120 million farmers with Rs. 20,000 crore allocated, stabilizing incomes and reducing production costs.

    Read More

  • Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana

    Launched in 2015, Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) promotes organic farming through financial assistance for farm conversion, training, and product promotion.

    It has brought 2 million hectares under organic cultivation, trained 1 million farmers, and created 10,000 jobs.

    Read More

  • Rashtriya Gokul Mission

    Launched in 2014, Rashtriya Gokul Mission (RGM) conserves and develops indigenous cattle breeds, improving milk production and genetic diversity through Gokul Grams and farmer training.

    It has established 100 Gokul Grams, trained 10,000 farmers, and increased indigenous cattle populations by 20%.

    Read More

  • Mission Amrit Sarovar

    Launched in 2022, Mission Amrit Sarovar aims to revive 75 ponds per district for irrigation, drinking water, and fish breeding, supporting groundwater replenishment and aquatic habitats.

    It involves local labor and resources with financial assistance from the Ministry of Jal Shakti.

    Read More

  • National Mission on Edible Oils

    The National Mission on Edible Oils – Oil Palm (NMEO-OP), launched in 2021, promotes oil palm cultivation to achieve self-sufficiency in edible oils by 2030.

    It provides incentives for farmers and supports oil palm processing infrastructure development.

    Read More

  • National Mission on Natural Farming

    Launched in 2021, the National Mission on Natural Farming (NMNF) promotes sustainable farming using natural inputs, aiming to increase natural farming area by 50% by 2025.

    It provides training, support, and financial aid to farmers adopting natural farming practices.

    Read More

  • National Scheme of Welfare of Fishermen

    The National Scheme of Welfare of Fishermen (NSWF), launched in 2006, improves fishermen's socio-economic conditions through subsidies, insurance, and training.

    It is implemented by the Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying.

    Read More

  • Pandit Deen Dayal Upadhyay Unnat Krishi Shiksha Yojana (PDDUUKSY)

    Pandit Deen Dayal Upadhyay Unnat Krishi Shiksha Yojana (PDDUUKSY) is a government scheme launched in 2016 to promote organic farming, natural farming, and a cow-based economy for environmental sustenance and soil health. The scheme is implemented by the Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR).

    The objective of the PDDUUKSY is to develop human resources in organic farming, natural farming, and a cow-based economy for environmental sustenance and soil health. The scheme is also aimed at promoting sustainable agriculture practices and reducing the use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides.

    The PDDUUKSY has been a major success since its launch. In the first five years of the scheme, over 1 million farmers have been trained in organic farming, natural farming, and cow-based economy. The scheme has also helped to establish over 10,000 organic farming, natural farming, and cow-based economy training centres.
