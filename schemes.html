Agricultural Schemes

  • Pradhan Mantri KISAN Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)

    The Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN) Yojana is a farmer welfare scheme launched by the Government of India on February 24, 2019. Under this scheme, the government provides a direct benefit of Rs 6,000 per year to all small and marginal farmers in the country. This benefit is provided in three installments of Rs 2,000 each every 4 months.

    Eligibility: All landholding farmers' families with cultivable land up to 2 hectares. Excluded categories include institutional landholders, farmer families holding constitutional posts, income tax payers, and government employees.

    Implementation: Over 11 crore farmers have been covered under the scheme with more than Rs 2.6 lakh crore disbursed as of 2024. The scheme uses Aadhaar-based beneficiary identification and Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) mechanism.

    Recent Updates: The scheme has been extended to all farmers regardless of landholding size and includes provisions for crop insurance linkage.

  • Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana

    The Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) is a crop insurance scheme launched by the Government of India on January 13, 2016, replacing earlier crop insurance schemes. It provides comprehensive insurance coverage against crop loss to farmers.

    Premium Structure: Farmers pay only 2% premium for Kharif crops, 1.5% for Rabi crops, and 5% for annual commercial/horticultural crops. The remaining premium is subsidized equally by Central and State governments.

    Coverage: Covers pre-sowing to post-harvest losses due to natural calamities, pests, diseases, and adverse weather conditions. Also covers localized calamities like hailstorm, landslide, and inundation.

    Technology Integration: Uses satellite imagery, drone technology, and smartphone applications for crop cutting experiments and loss assessment to ensure quick claim settlement.

    Achievement: Over 36 crore farmer applications enrolled with claims worth over Rs 1.2 lakh crore paid since inception.

  • Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana

    The Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY) was launched in 2015 with the motto "Har Khet Ko Pani" (Water to Every Field). The scheme aims to achieve convergence of investments in irrigation at the field level.

    Components: (1) Accelerated Irrigation Benefits Programme (AIBP), (2) Har Khet Ko Pani, (3) Per Drop More Crop, and (4) Watershed Development.

    Financial Assistance: 55% subsidy for drip/sprinkler irrigation for small farmers, 45% for other farmers, and 55% for all categories in northeastern states and scheduled areas.

    Targets: Creation of irrigation potential for 76.03 lakh hectares, water conservation, and improving water use efficiency through micro-irrigation.

    Achievements: Over 69 lakh hectares brought under micro-irrigation coverage with water saving of 40-60% and productivity increase of 35-40%.

  • Ayushman Sahakar Scheme

    The Ayushman Sahakar Scheme was launched in 2021 to support cooperatives in creating healthcare infrastructure in rural and underserved areas. It's implemented by the National Cooperative Development Corporation (NCDC).

    Financial Support: Term loan up to Rs 10 crore per project with 1% interest subvention. Projects include hospitals, diagnostic centers, medical colleges, nursing schools, and ambulance services.

    Eligible Cooperatives: Primary, state, multi-state, and national level cooperatives with a minimum 3-year track record and sound financial position.

    Coverage: Focus on Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities, rural areas, and underserved regions to improve healthcare accessibility and affordability.

  • E-NAM (National Agriculture Market)

    eNAM, or the National Agriculture Market, is a pan-India electronic trading portal launched on April 14, 2016, to create a unified national market for agricultural commodities by networking 1,000+ existing APMC mandis.

    Features: Online trading, transparent price discovery, real-time information on prices and arrivals, online payment and settlement, quality assaying infrastructure, and storage facilities.

    Benefits: Better price discovery for farmers, reduced transaction costs, elimination of information asymmetry, promotion of competitive and efficient markets.

    Current Status: 1,361 mandis across 23 states and 3 UTs integrated with average daily trade value exceeding Rs 100 crore. Over 1.8 crore farmers and 2.5 lakh traders registered.

    Technology: Mobile app available in 8 languages, integration with Unified Payment Interface (UPI), and quality testing facilities at mandi level.

  • Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maan-Dhan Yojana (PM-KMY)

    Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maandhan Yojana (PM-KMY) is a voluntary pension scheme launched on 12 September 2019 for small and marginal farmers. It provides a guaranteed monthly pension of Rs. 3,000 after age 60 for farmers with up to 2 hectares of land, with contributions matched by the government.

    Farmers contribute Rs. 55 to Rs. 200 monthly, and the government matches up to Rs. 1,000. The scheme ensures financial security in old age.

  • Krishi Kalyan Abhiyan

    Krishi Kalyan Abhiyan (KKA), launched in 2018, aims to improve farmers' income and livelihood in 112 aspirational districts. Activities include distributing soil health cards, livestock vaccination, mini kits, custom hiring centres, organic farming, and farmer training.

    It has trained over 11 lakh farmers, conducted 5000 demonstrations, and improved soil health on 15 lakh hectares.

  • Soil Health Cards (SHC) Scheme

    The Soil Health Card (SHC) Scheme, launched in 2015, provides farmers with soil nutrient analysis and recommendations to improve soil health and productivity. Soil samples are tested for 12 parameters, including nitrogen, phosphorus, and pH.

    It helps farmers identify nutrient deficiencies, save on fertilizers, increase crop productivity, and protect the environment.

  • National Bamboo Mission

    The National Bamboo Mission (NBM) promotes bamboo cultivation and industry development. It provides financial assistance for plantations, nurseries, farmer training, and bamboo-based industries.

    It has brought over 1 lakh hectares under bamboo cultivation, trained 1 lakh farmers, and created 10,000 jobs.

  • Green Revolution – Krishonnati Yojana

    Launched in 2005, the Krishonnati Yojana aims to uplift agriculture through 11 schemes, including horticulture development, sustainable agriculture, mechanization, and e-governance, to improve farmer incomes and production.

    It focuses on scientific approaches, nutritional security, and agricultural marketing infrastructure.

  • Pradhan Mantri Annadata Aay SanraksHan Abhiyan (PM-AASHA)

    Launched in 2018, PM-AASHA provides price support to farmers for crops like wheat, rice, pulses, and oilseeds, ensuring a minimum price based on production costs and market prices.

    It benefits around 120 million farmers with Rs. 20,000 crore allocated, stabilizing incomes and reducing production costs.

  • Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana

    Launched in 2015, Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) promotes organic farming through financial assistance for farm conversion, training, and product promotion.

    It has brought 2 million hectares under organic cultivation, trained 1 million farmers, and created 10,000 jobs.

  • Rashtriya Gokul Mission

    Launched in December 2014, Rashtriya Gokul Mission (RGM) is a comprehensive program for conservation and development of indigenous cattle breeds to enhance milk production and productivity of bovines.

    Objectives: Conservation of indigenous cattle and buffalo breeds, genetic improvement through selective breeding, establishment of integrated cattle development centers (Gokul Grams), and promotion of artificial insemination with high genetic merit bulls.

    Key Components: Establishment of Gokul Grams as integrated cattle development centers, breed improvement programs, distribution of high genetic merit bulls, strengthening of breeding infrastructure, and field performance recording.

    Achievements: 28 Gokul Grams sanctioned, over 10,000 farmers trained, genetic improvement in indigenous breeds achieved, and increased milk productivity by 15-20% in covered areas.

  • Mission Amrit Sarovar

    Launched on April 24, 2022, on the occasion of Akshaya Tritiya, Mission Amrit Sarovar is a comprehensive water conservation initiative to develop and rejuvenate 75 water bodies in each district of the country, totaling approximately 50,000 Amrit Sarovars.

    Objectives: Water conservation and security, groundwater recharge, biodiversity conservation, improvement in water table, income generation for rural population, and climate resilience building.

    Implementation Strategy: District collectors as nodal officers, involvement of Panchayati Raj Institutions, use of geo-tagging and drone technology for monitoring, and Jan Bhagidari (people's participation) approach.

    Progress: Over 50,000 Amrit Sarovars completed or under construction across 732 districts. Emphasis on completing work before monsoon season for maximum water storage benefit.

  • National Mission on Edible Oils

    The National Mission on Edible Oils – Oil Palm (NMEO-OP), launched in August 2021 with an outlay of Rs 11,040 crore, aims to increase domestic production of edible oils and reduce import dependency from 85% to 50% by 2030.

    Incentive Structure: Rs 29,000 per hectare in 2 installments for oil palm cultivation, Rs 12,000 per hectare assistance for intercropping during gestation period, and Rs 8,000 per hectare for maintenance.

    Processing Support: 50% subsidy for setting up oil palm processing units, strengthening existing mills, and establishing collection centers.

    Target Areas: Focus on northeastern states, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Odisha, West Bengal, and Assam with assured irrigation facilities.

    Expected Outcomes: 10 lakh hectare area expansion, 16.7 lakh MT additional production, and generation of 75 lakh person-days employment annually.

  • National Mission on Natural Farming

    Launched in 2021, the National Mission on Natural Farming (NMNF) promotes chemical-free agriculture using natural inputs and traditional farming practices, aiming to reduce input costs and improve soil health.

    Key Principles: Zero Budget Natural Farming (ZBNF), use of cow-based preparations, elimination of chemical fertilizers and pesticides, and promotion of indigenous seeds and traditional knowledge.

    Financial Support: Rs 50,000 per hectare for 3 years for farmers adopting natural farming, support for training programs, and assistance for organic certification.

    Implementation: Formation of farmer clusters, training through master trainers, demonstration plots, and convergence with existing schemes like PMKSY and Soil Health Card scheme.

  • National Scheme of Welfare of Fishermen

    The National Scheme of Welfare of Fishermen (NSWF), launched in 2006 and restructured in 2018, aims to improve the socio-economic conditions of fishermen and their families through various welfare measures.

    Key Components: Savings-cum-relief scheme during fishing ban period, group accident insurance, construction of community halls, providing communication equipment, and training in alternative livelihood activities.

    Financial Assistance: Rs 6,000 per family during closed fishing season, group accident insurance of Rs 1 lakh per fisherman, and subsidized diesel for fishing boats.

    Infrastructure Support: Construction of fish landing centers, ice plants, cold storage facilities, fish markets, and boat repair yards to strengthen fishing infrastructure.

  • Pandit Deen Dayal Upadhyay Unnat Krishi Shiksha Yojana (PDDUUKSY)

    Pandit Deen Dayal Upadhyay Unnat Krishi Shiksha Yojana (PDDUUKSY) is a government scheme launched in 2016 to promote organic farming, natural farming, and a cow-based economy for environmental sustenance and soil health. The scheme is implemented by the Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR).

    The objective of the PDDUUKSY is to develop human resources in organic farming, natural farming, and a cow-based economy for environmental sustenance and soil health. The scheme is also aimed at promoting sustainable agriculture practices and reducing the use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides.

    The PDDUUKSY has been a major success since its launch. In the first five years of the scheme, over 1 million farmers have been trained in organic farming, natural farming, and cow-based economy. The scheme has also helped to establish over 10,000 organic farming, natural farming, and cow-based economy training centres.

  • Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Scheme

    The Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Scheme was introduced in 1998-99 to provide adequate and timely credit to farmers for their cultivation and other needs. It's a revolutionary credit delivery mechanism for the agricultural sector.

    Credit Limit: Based on operational landholding, cropping pattern, and scale of finance. Sublimit of 10% of production credit for post-harvest/household/consumption requirements.

    Features: Flexible repayment schedule aligned with harvesting and marketing cycles, revolving credit facility, minimal documentation, and crop insurance coverage.

    Interest Rate: 7% per annum with 3% interest subvention by government, effectively 4% for prompt repayers. Additional 2% interest subvention for prompt repayment, making effective rate 2%.

    Coverage: Over 7 crore active KCC holders with outstanding credit of Rs 7 lakh crore. Recent expansion to include fisheries and animal husbandry sectors.

  • Formation and Promotion of Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs)

    The Formation and Promotion of 10,000 FPOs scheme was approved in February 2020 with a budget of Rs 6,865 crore to form and promote Farmer Producer Organizations across the country by 2027-28.

    Structure: Minimum 300 members for plain areas and 100 members for hilly/tribal areas. Each FPO receives financial support of Rs 18.00 lakh over 3 years for business development.

    Support System: Cluster-Based Business Organizations (CBBOs) provide professional handholding support for 5 years. Professional agencies ensure technical, financial, and marketing support.

    Benefits: Enhanced bargaining power, reduced input costs, better market access, value addition opportunities, and collective farming benefits.

    Progress: Over 8,000 FPOs formed covering 1.2 crore farmers with business turnover exceeding Rs 5,000 crore annually.

  • Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization (SMAM)

    The Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization (SMAM) promotes farm mechanization through financial assistance, custom hiring centers, and technology demonstrations to increase agricultural productivity and reduce drudgery.

    Financial Assistance: 40-50% subsidy on agricultural machinery and equipment for individual farmers, 80% for demonstrations, and up to Rs 10 lakh for establishing Custom Hiring Centers (CHCs).

    Key Components: Promotion of agricultural machinery, establishment of Farm Machinery Banks/CHCs, creation of hubs for hi-tech equipment, and training programs for operators and entrepreneurs.

    Priority: Special focus on small and marginal farmers, women farmers, SC/ST farmers, and farmers in northeastern states with higher subsidy rates.

    Achievements: Over 15,000 CHCs established, 4 lakh farmers trained, and mechanization level increased from 40% to 47% during 2014-2022.
